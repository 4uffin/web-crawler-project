Log in to hide these messages.Every Winner From The 2025 Primetime Emmys: The Complete ListEvery Winner From The 2025 Primetime Emmys: The Complete ListBy Kelby VeraSenior ReporterSep 14, 2025, 08:11 PM EDTLEAVE A COMMENTLOADINGERROR LOADINGThe spotlight was on the small screen during the 77th Primetime Emmy Awards on Sunday.Hosted by comedian Nate Bargatze, the ceremony honors television’s best at the Peacock Theater in downtown Los Angeles.The evening’s top contenders came from streaming platforms and premium cable, with just a few traditional network series in the mix.Advertisement
Apple TV+’s enigmatic workplace drama “Severance” scored 27 total nominations for its head-spinning second season. Its biggest competition is HBO Max’s edge-of-your-seat hospital drama, “The Pitt,” according to critics.Leading in the comedy categories is another Apple TV+ production: the hysterical Hollywood send-up “The Studio.” It nabbed 23 nods, tying the Emmy record for the most nominations in a single year for a comedy series.“The Studio’s” fiercest competition is another knee-slapper with industry insider appeal: last Emmys’ most-awarded comedy, “Hacks.”Advertisement
View of Emmy statuettes at the 77th Primetime Emmy Awards at Los Angeles' Peacock Theater on Sunday.Frazer Harrison via Getty ImagesWith 24 nominations, HBO Max’s dark, DC Comics-based crime drama “The Penguin” topped the limited series set. Its in a tight race with the Netflix miniseries “Adolescence,” a gripping dive into a teen boy who murders his classmate. Many of this year’s trophies already were doled out during last weekend’s Creative Arts Emmys, where 47 categories focusing on outstanding artistic and technical achievements in television were announced.Advertisement
Sunday night will focus on a range of acting, writing and directing honors, as well as awards for the top drama, comedy, talk show and reality competition.Hollywood power couple Ted Danson and Mary Steenburgen will receive the 2025 Bob Hope Humanitarian Award.The 77th Primetime Emmys are being broadcast by CBS starting at 8 p.m. ET/5 p.m. PT. You can stream the show live with Paramount+’s premium plan or through any services that carry CBS.Advertisement
