Relatives of a teenage girl whose remains were found in a car registered to the singer D4vd have said they are "heartbroken."The body found in D4vd's Tesla was identified as 15-year-old Celeste Rivas, who was reported missing last year.Newsweek contacted the Los Angeles Police Department for comment via email on Thursday outside regular working hours.The ContextRivas, from Lake Elsinore, California, went missing on April 5, 2024, at the age of 13, about 17 months before her body was found.On September 8, a decomposing body was discovered in D4vd's Tesla and was later identified as Rivas. The Los Angeles County Medical Examiner's Office said she had been dead for "a long time." Her cause of death has not been determined.D4vd is a 20-year-old singer with over 33 million monthly listeners on Spotify and 3.7 million followers on TikTok. He released his debut album earlier this year and was on tour when Rivas' body was found.What To KnowIn a GoFundMe page, Rivas' family said the discovered remains had been identified as the teenager."She was a beloved daughter, sister, cousin, and friend. Her family is heartbroken and devastated by this tragic loss," they said in a statement.The fundraiser, launched on Thursday, is seeking donations to "help to lay her body to rest." About $11,000 had been raised as of Friday morning.

The medical examiner's office said that when the remains were discovered, the victim was wearing a tube top, black leggings, stud earrings and a yellow bracelet.Rivas was 5-foot-1 and weighed about 71 pounds. She had black hair and a tattoo on her right index finger that said, "Shhh."The LAPD searched a house linked to D4vd, whose real name is David Anthony Burke, overnight on Wednesday, ABC reported.Law enforcement sources told the outlet that the musician had been staying at the house but did not own it.ABC reported that detectives were seen taking a computer and other items from the home."Several items of evidence were recovered and will be analyzed by detectives in the coming days," the LAPD said in a statement to ABC News. "This is an ongoing investigation. Investigators are following up on several leads."What People Are SayingA spokesperson for D4vd told NBC on Tuesday: "D4vd has been informed about what's happened. And, although he is still out on tour, he is fully cooperating with the authorities."The Los Angeles Police Department said in a statement to NBC earlier this week: "At this time, the LAPD does not have a crime classification from the coroner as to the mode or manner of death. Thus, we do not have any suspect information at this time."What Happens NextThe investigation into Rivas' death is ongoing.
