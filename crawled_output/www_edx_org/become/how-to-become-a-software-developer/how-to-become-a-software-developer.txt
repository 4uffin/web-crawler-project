Plan your career, and make the most of your education by learning how to become a standout software developer.What does a software developer do?Software developers' responsibilities can vary depending on their employer and specific job title. However, these professionals can generally expect to perform the same day-to-day tasks: working with stakeholders to set goals, coding new software systems, and testing applications for bugs. They may also perform quality assurance tests and update software throughout its lifecycle.The Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) reports that software developers earned a median salary of $131,450 as of May 2024. The BLS projects jobs for these professionals to grow by 16% between 2024 and 2034, much higher than the average 3% growth projected across all occupations. You may graduate sooner if you already hold an associate degree or took Advanced Placement courses in high school. Work with undergraduate academic advisors to create a graduation timeline that meets your schedule.What coding languages should I know to become a software developer?The best coding languages to know for software development include:JavaScriptPythonJavaPHPKoltinIntroductory edX and first-year college courses can introduce you to these and other in-demand languages. Master those that best align with your academic interests and career goals.What's the difference between a software developer and a software engineer?Software developers program individual applications, while software engineers build and maintain the software architecture supporting those applications. Although their responsibilities vary, professionals in these roles collaborate on projects and have a similar educational background. College career advisors can help you explore these and related jobs.What's the difference between software developers and web developers?Web developers specialize in website design, while software developers create applications for electronic devices. Web development also requires greater collaboration with outside stakeholders, such as clients commissioning a website. Software developers spend most of their time working alongside peers and managers within the same company or organization.Can I become a software developer without a degree?Although some entry-level careers require only college certificates or professional certifications, a bachelor's degree can qualify you for more positions and may help you earn a higher starting salary.
