If you enjoy posting on social media and interacting with others through various platforms, a career as a social media manager could be a good fit for you. In this guide, you'll discover the steps to take to get started in this field.Browse online social media programs and coursesWhat does a social media manager do?Social media managers' responsibilities typically include:Creating, curating, and managing content for clientsStaying current on the various social media platformsEngaging with the audience by responding to comments, posting content, encouraging interaction, and dealing with negative online postsDeveloping the overarching strategy for the brand and creating brand consistency across platformsSetting up social media marketing campaigns, analyzing campaign results, and adjusting the campaigns to maximize resultsYou can find work as a social media manager in a wide range of industries. For example, you can work for a digital marketing agency, in a corporate setting, or as a freelancer. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), social media managers (categorized by the BLS as public relations specialists) earned a median salary of $66,750 as of May 2023.Steps to become a social media managerThe field of social media management does not have strict education or certification requirements like many other industries. Some professionals may find success as social media managers by building their own platforms and making a name for themselves without any formal education. To stand out from the competition and maximize your chances of landing a social media manager position, however, investing in your education and gaining practical experience are key. Below are five steps to consider:Step 1: Earn a relevant degree or get trainingA degree in marketing, journalism, communications, or a related field can provide a strong foundation for a career in social media management. These programs help you build skills essential for success in this role, including communication, digital marketing, and strategic thinking.Alternatively, you could consider completing online courses that teach digital marketing fundamentals. The good news is that you can find high-quality training in social media management without taking the traditional route of completing a four-year bachelor's degree program.Step 2: Find an internshipAn internship may provide practical and hands-on experience in social media marketing. Look for opportunities with marketing agencies, startups, or small businesses. In an internship, you may work on actual campaigns, learn about different social media platforms, and develop your understanding of brand voice and audience engagement.Some benefits of internships can include:Learning from experienced social media professionalsGaining experience working with popular platforms like Instagram, Facebook, and LinkedInDeveloping your portfolio by showcasing successful campaigns you helped createStep 3: Build a strong online presenceA critical step to becoming a successful social media manager is establishing your presence online. Create and maintain professional social media accounts where you share industry insights, create content, and highlight successful campaigns you set up for clients. Demonstrating your expertise through your own channels can help you stand out to potential employers.Tips for building your online presence include:Sharing articles, videos, and content related to social media trendsEngaging with industry leaders and participating in relevant discussionsUsing platforms like LinkedIn to network and showcase your knowledgeStep 4: Pursue entry-level positionsYou will most likely start your career as a social media manager in an entry-level position. Look for positions such as a social media associate, content creator, or digital marketing assistant.Starting your career in a position where you assist and learn from a social media manager can provide you with valuable experience in creating content, managing social accounts, and analyzing performance metrics. This type of position can provide you with a solid foundation for your future career as a social media manager.Step 5: Obtain certificates to stay currentCertificates can boost your credibility and showcase your skills. Consider the MIT Sloan School of Management's Social Media Strategy Certificate.You can also continue your learning throughout your career by attending webinars, taking online courses, and following social media thought leaders to stay updated. Social media is constantly evolving, so ongoing professional development is essential.Get started on edXReady to be a social media manager?
