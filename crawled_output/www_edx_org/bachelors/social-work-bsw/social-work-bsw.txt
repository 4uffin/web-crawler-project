With a focus on field work and practical skills, BSW programs offer students a hands-on approach to higher education.Explore BSW programsWhat jobs can you get with an online bachelor's in social work?With a BSW degree, you can pursue non-clinical roles such as social and community service manager, health education specialist, or probation officer.Social workers interested in clinical roles, such as mental health therapists or substance use counselors, must earn a master's in social work and work toward clinical licensure.Salary expectations and career outlook for social workersAmong BSW and MSW degree holders, social workers earned a median annual salary of $58,380 in 2023, according to the Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS).Clinical social workers and healthcare social workers with master's in social work (MSW) degrees typically earn the highest salaries in social work. The Bureau reports that jobs for social workers are expected to grow by 7% from 2023-33, which is faster than the 4% projected across all occupations.Below are a few non-clinical roles you can pursue with a BSW, with median annual salaries from the BLS:Jobs you can get with a BSWJobMedian annual salaryHealth education specialist$62,860Social and community service managers$77,030Probation officer$61,800What can you learn in an online bachelor's in social work program?Most BSW programs require you to complete 46-48 credits in social work courses and a 400-hour practicum that offers hands-on experience in the field under the supervision of an experienced social work supervisor.Core program courses focus on human behavior, diversity and cultural understanding, case management, and crisis intervention. Practice-oriented courses focus on work in child welfare or with other populations.An online bachelor's in social work program can equip you with the knowledge, skills, and experience you need to launch a meaningful career post-graduation.Online formatCompleting a bachelor's of social work online can be convenient for students balancing work, school, and family responsibilities with coursework.To complete your degree program online, you will need a computer and high-speed internet. Online coursework typically includes self-paced learning modules, written assignments, and face-to-face video conferencing for group discussion or individual instruction.Some fieldwork activities may also be completed remotely, though field placement requirements vary widely. Expect your field placement practicum to be completed in person.If you currently work at a social services agency, you may be able to apply for an employment-based field placement.
