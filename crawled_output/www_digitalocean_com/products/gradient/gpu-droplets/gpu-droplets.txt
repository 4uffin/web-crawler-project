With GPU Droplets, you can run training and inference on AI/ML models, process large data sets and complex neural networks for deep learning use cases, and serve additional use cases like high-performance computing (HPC).What are some of the benefits of using GPU Droplets with DigitalOcean?Scalable, on-demand GPU computeVirtual instances to manage costSeamless integration with the broader DigitalOcean ecosystem, including access to our Kubernetes serviceFlexible configurations from single-GPU to 8-GPU setupsPre-installed Python and Deep Learning software packagesAll GPU Droplets are HIPAA-eligible and SOC 2 compliantWhat regions are GPU Droplets available in?GPU Droplets are available in our New York, Atlanta, and Toronto data centers.Do you provide an SLA for GPU Droplets?DigitalOcean provides a 99.5% uptime SLA for GPU Droplets.Will I be billed for powered-off GPU Droplets?When you power off your GPU Droplet, you are still billed for it. This is because your disk space, CPU, RAM, and IP address are all reserved, even while powered off. Therefore, charges are made until you destroy the instance.How are GPU Droplets billed?GPU Droplets are billed per second with a 5-minute minimum billing period.What if I use my GPU Droplet for less than a month, would I still be charged for the whole month?No. You will only be charged for the time you use your GPU Droplet in seconds. If you use your GPU Droplet for more than the standard 672-hour monthly limit, you will still be charged for the time you use your GPU Droplets in seconds.Where can I see prices for GPU Droplets?Visit our pricing page for more information.Why are scratch disks not included in some GPU Droplets?Scratch disks are geared towards large model training use cases. GPUs that are more appropriate for inference and graphics do not include a scratch disk.
