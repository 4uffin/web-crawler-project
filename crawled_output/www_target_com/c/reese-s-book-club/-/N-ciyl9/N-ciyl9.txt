When Steph was five, her mother fled an abusive husband—taking her two daughters to Cherokee Nation, a place she felt a claim to. In response, Steph sets her sights far from Oklahoma, vowing nothing will stop her from becoming the first Cherokee astronaut. Her relentless quest to reach the moon irrevocably alters the fates of those she loves.
- shows more contentshopAbout the authorEliana Ramage holds an MFA in fiction from the Iowa Writers’ Workshop. She has received residencies and fellowships from the Kimmel Harding Nelson Center, Lambda Literary, Tin House and Vermont Studio Center. A citizen of the Cherokee Nation, she lives in Nashville with her family. To the Moon and Back is her debut novel.Why we love this pickIn Steph’s certainty that only her ambition can save her, she stretches her bonds to the point of breaking, betraying their love and forcing them to reconsider their own deepest desires. To the Moon and Back is an astounding and expansive novel of mothers and daughters, love and sacrifice, terror and wonder.
